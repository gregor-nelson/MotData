MOT History Check

Check the vehicle's MOT history

These are 'dangerous' defects that cause immediate MOT failure. If present in recent tests, investigate further before purchasing.

  • Tyre tread depth not in accordance with the requirements Tyres
  • A tyre seriously damaged Tyres
  • Brake disc or drum excessively weakened, insecure or fractured Brakes
  • a brake lining or pad worn below 1.5mm Brakes
  • A tyre cords visible or damaged Tyres
  • Parking brake efficiency less than 50% of the required value Brakes
  • A seat belt webbing or flexible stalk significantly stretched or weakened Seat belts and supplementary restraint systems
  • A suspension pin, bush or joint likely to become detached Suspension
  • A tyre has a lump, bulge or tear caused by separation or partial failure of its structure. This includes any lifting of the tread rubber Tyres
  • Stop lamps all missing or inoperative Lamps, reflectors and electrical equipment
  • A tyre valve seriously damaged or misaligned likely to cause sudden deflation of the tyre Tyres
  • Fire risk due to fuel tank shield or exhaust shield missing where fitted as original equipment Body, chassis, structure
  • A wheel bearing so rough it is likely to overheat or break up Suspension
  • Leaking brake pipe or connection on a hydraulic system Brakes
  • The strength or continuity of the load bearing structure within 30cm of any sub-frame, spring or suspension component mounting (a 'prescribed area') is so weakened that control of the vehicle is likely to be adversely affected Suspension
  • Exhaust emits excessive smoke or vapour of any colour to an extent likely to obscure the vision of other road users. Noise, emissions and leaks
  • Brake hose damaged and likely to fail Brakes
  • A wheel bearing play so excessive it is likely to break up or directional control impaired Suspension
  • Brakes imbalance across an axle such that the braking effort from any wheel is less than 50% of the maximum effort recorded from the other wheel on a steered axle Brakes
  • Bumper likely to become detached Body, chassis, structure
  • A suspension component missing, likely to become detached or directional stability impaired Suspension
  • A shock absorber missing or likely to become detached Suspension
  • A steering ball joint worn to the extent there is a serious risk of detachment Steering
  • Braking effort not recording at any wheel Brakes
  • Steering gear casing retaining devices dangerously loose or relative movement to chassis/bodywork visible Steering
